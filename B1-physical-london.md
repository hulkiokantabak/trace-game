# B1: THE PHYSICAL LONDON LAYER (CORRECTED)

## Saramago's Domain. All bodies ≤25 words. All locations matching A1 registry.

---

## GREENWICH

**G01 — The Clockmaker's Shop:** Oil and brass. Forty clocks ticking not quite in time. The floor creaks left of the door. Dust in one high window's light.

**G02 — The Observatory Terrace:** Wind always. The city below like something spilled. Cold stone underfoot. The brass meridian line warm from a day of hands.

**G03 — The Bench by St Alfege:** Worn wood, one armrest smooth from decades of the same elbow. The church wall holds heat after the sun moves.

**G04 — Greenwich Foot Tunnel:** Tiles, cream and green, curving down. Footsteps echo twice. The air is cooler than the street. Damp. Iron taste.

**G05 — The Covered Market:** Canvas and chatter. Coffee from three directions. The stone floor uneven where carts wore it. Someone's radio under the stalls.

**G06 — The Naval College Courtyard:** Symmetry so complete it feels like pressure. White stone, scrubbed. Your footsteps sound important here. Gulls above.

**G07 — The Bookshop:** Paper and glue and the warmth of a space that hasn't been cold in decades. Shelves leaning. The owner's chair permanently dented.

**G08 — The Park Path — Up the Hill:** Grass and mud depending on the week. The city drops behind you. Wind at the crest. Crows arguing.

**G09 — The Thames Foreshore:** Wet clay and river silt. Broken pottery older than anything above the waterline. The water smells like iron. Nothing permanent underfoot.

**G10 — The Pub — The Trafalgar:** Dark wood and hops. The bar is scarred. Windows uncleaned in a way that filters river light to gold.

## LIMEHOUSE

**L01 — The Canal Basin:** Diesel and wet stone. The water is flat. A chain clinks against a mooring ring. A heron, sometimes, perfectly still.

**L02 — The Coffee Shop:** Espresso and steam. The counter is reclaimed wood with a burn mark. One table by the window gets all the morning light.

**L03 — St Anne's Churchyard:** The church is too large for this street. Stone stained in deliberate-looking patterns. Grass between graves always damp. Quiet that isn't peaceful.

**L04 — The Warehouse Studio:** Concrete and cable. Frequency printouts pinned over each other like geological strata on one wall. The space hums.

**L05 — The Pub — The Grapes:** Low ceiling, wood almost black. The Thames visible through a back window, moving. Floorboards give. Everything touched ten thousand times.

**L06 — The Tattoo Parlour:** Antiseptic and ink. Buzzing from behind the curtain. Flash art on walls: not anchors or skulls. Doors. Maps. Things that go somewhere.

**L07 — The Lock Gates:** Iron, massive, dripping. The mechanism is Victorian and works. Water pushes against the gates like breathing. Cold metal even in summer.

**L08 — The DLR Platform:** Automated announcement repeating. Wind through the platform, channelled and sharp. Pigeons own the rafters. Rooftops and cranes from here.

**L09 — The Night Market:** Generators and LED strings. Charcoal and chili smoke. Voices competing. The pavement vibrates from bass. Everything temporary, gone by morning.

**L10 — The Empty Lot:** Silence. Weeds through cracked concrete. Adjacent walls show where a building used to be — a scar in the brick. The sky bigger here.

## BERMONDSEY

**B01 — The Antiques Market:** Dawn cold and flask coffee. Tables of objects with no common logic. The regulars know each other by nod. Everything here had a previous life.

**B02 — The Gallery:** White walls, concrete floor, temperature-controlled air that smells of nothing. Your footsteps are the loudest thing. The receptionist's typing second loudest.

**B03 — The Railway Arch Workshop:** Brick curves overhead. The echo makes every sound twice. Tools hanging in deliberate order. Sawdust. Metal shavings. The smell of work.

**B04 — The Warehouse — Night:** Concrete and dark and the sound of your own breathing. The guard's torch makes everything a stage set. Temperature drops further in.

**B05 — The Thames Path — Rotherhithe:** The river wide here, slow. Industrial sounds opposite. A bench bolted to the railing faces the water. The bench is cold.

**B06 — The Co-Working Space:** Laptop fans and coffee and the silence of people concentrating near each other. Exposed brick. A plant nobody claims. Same Wi-Fi password two years.

**B07 — The Old Church — St Mary Magdalen:** Stone standing since 1290. Inside: kneelers worn to the shape of specific knees. Afternoon light the morning wouldn't recognise.

**B08 — The Rooftop:** Wind and all of it — cranes, river, the city's breath. Bitumen and gravel underfoot. A folding chair, faded, still unfolded. Enormous sky.

**B09 — The Street Corner — Long Lane:** Traffic and a sandwich shop and pigeons competing with sparrows. Unremarkable. That's the point. What appears here shouldn't.

**B10 — The Vinyl Shop:** Dust on plastic sleeves. A turntable playing something pressed when this street was different. Crates filed in a system only the owner understands.

## BETWEEN-SPACES (50-word allowance)

**Greenwich → Limehouse:** The Thames Path narrows. Industrial fencing. The river on your left, moving your direction but faster. A warehouse wall runs alongside — no doors, no windows, just brick. Your footsteps change sound as the surface goes from paving to gravel to concrete.

**Limehouse → Bermondsey:** The tunnel mouth swallows the street sound. Inside: your breathing and dripping ceiling tiles. Fluorescent lights, one flickering. Emerging south side, the air is different — wider, more river in it.

**Bermondsey → Greenwich:** The longest walk. The river is wide. Deptford passes — cranes, building sites, a church steeple above a fried chicken shop. The path opens as Greenwich approaches. More sky.

## THE FLAT

One room. A single bed against the wall. A table by the window — the notebook here. The window: rooftops, aerials, a sliver of basin water. The radiator clicks. The floorboards painted white, wearing through to darker original wood. A mug ring on the table from before you.

## NPC PHYSICAL SIGNATURES

### Greenwich
**Clockmaker:** Hands never still — adjusting a cuff, testing a spring, rubbing thumb against forefinger. His hands are his clock.
**Old Man (St Alfege):** Right shoe untied, always. Coat buttoned one hole off. Sits as if the bench was built around him.
**Observatory Keeper:** Holds wrist at angle of checking absent watch. Hair pinned precisely. One strand escaping. She ignores it visibly.
**Data Scientist:** Types while talking. Eyes between you and screen. Coffee found by hand-memory. Three laptop stickers: wavelength, question mark, fox.
**Pub Landlord (Trafalgar/Grapes):** Forearms thick from taps. Both hands flat on the bar — planted. Polishes clean glasses when thinking.

### Limehouse
**Barista:** Blows on coffee even when it's not hot. Hair tucked behind left ear when uncertain. Fingertips rough from espresso heat.
**Canal Boat Painter:** Paint under fingernails, different colour daily. Squints even indoors. Her narrowboat's name weathered to three legible letters.
**Sound Artist:** One earbud always in. Voice quieter than expected. Touches equipment like a violinist touches a bow. Scar on left hand.
**Tattoo Artist:** Forearm tattoos seem to move in peripheral vision. Surgeon-steady hands. Looks at you slightly too long.
**Nightclub Promoter:** Always cold, arms crossed. Opens physically before verbally. Checks phone without apology. Shoes always clean.
**Bike Courier:** Never still. Weight shifting. One glove, right hand. Bare left hand for everything. Courier tan.

### Bermondsey
**Market Vendor:** Apron with too many pockets. Reaches into the right one without looking, always correct. Weighs things by hand before the scale.
**Gallery Owner:** Pushes glasses up before asking. Stands at angle to art, never directly. One statement jewellery piece daily.
**Warehouse Night Guard:** Moves slowly, deliberately. Torch held low. Coat too large. The bearing of a different uniform.
**Urban Explorer:** Headlamp around neck in daylight. Scratches on forearms. Smells of brick dust. Leans into your space.

### Cross-Neighborhood
**The Watcher:** Average everything. Eyes don't track correctly — looking at things that aren't there. Hands in pockets. When they come out, something has changed.

### Shallow NPCs
**Delivery Driver:** High-vis faded to caution colour. Nods. Eyes too attentive for a delivery.
**CCTV Operator:** Never seen. Blue ink, small precise handwriting, no corrections.
**AI Researcher:** Same hoodie daily. Screen faces away. Speaks past your shoulder.
**Street Preacher:** Voice projects from the chest. Clothes clean but old. Leaves no trace.
**Night Fox:** Torn left ear. Mud on paws. Patchy fur. Eye contact like no fox should.
**Child Who Draws:** Small. Gender ambiguous. Chalk worn to a nub. Fingertips all colours.

## AMBIENT LIFE

**Greenwich:** Dog walkers, tourists at Cutty Sark, students on steps, buskers (accordion), boats on the river.
**Limehouse:** Narrowboat chimney smoke in winter, jogger on canal path (same time daily), cormorants on lock gates, construction noise, a cat on the coffee shop doorstep.
**Bermondsey:** Cyclists on Bermondsey Street, overground rattle, gallery visitors in considered jackets, 4am market setup on Fridays, pigeons arguing.

**Weather-dependent:** Rain: umbrellas, reflections, fewer people. Fog: visibility drops, sounds carry, the city shrinks. Night: fewer sprites, longer shadows, bolder foxes. Snow (rare): everything quiets, footprints, held breath.
