## LEAVE LONDON — FINAL WALK NARRATION

*These short text blocks appear during the final walk through each neighborhood. Not dialogue — atmosphere. The city seen one last time.*

**Greenwich leg:**
"Greenwich in whatever light it happens to be. The observatory on the hill. The park. The foot tunnel entrance, round and certain. The clockmaker's shop is closed or open and either way the clocks tick. The bench is empty or occupied. The river beyond the market, moving toward the sea the way it has for longer than London has had a name."

**Limehouse leg:**
"The canal. The basin. The narrow streets with their converted warehouses and their unconverted ones. The coffee shop window with its one good table and its morning light. The church that doesn't fit its street, that never did, that was built for a congregation of something other than people. Your flat, from outside. The window. Someone else will live there. They won't hear what you heard."

**Bermondsey leg:**
"The arches. The market square, empty or full depending on the day. The gallery's white walls. The warehouse — even now, even in daylight, it watches you pass. The street corner where the preacher stood. The Thames path. The river. The same river. Always the same river."

## EVENT ENDING NARRATION

### Musician Endings

**M-END-1: "The Full Recording"**
"You played it. The complete recording. Two thousand years of London's sound — every voice, every footstep, every bell, every engine, every whisper in every alley — compressed into a single sustained frequency that bloomed across the city like dawn.

The barista stopped humming. She didn't need to anymore. Everyone could hear it.

The clockmaker's clocks all aligned — every one, including the stopped one — ticking in unison for the first time in two hundred years. Then they began to sing.

The Sound Artist's equipment overloaded. She laughed. She cried. She said: 'Finally.'

London is loud now. London is everything it ever was, all at once, and nobody can unhear it. Whether that's a gift or a catastrophe depends on who you ask. The barista says gift. The Watcher says catastrophe. The city itself says nothing. It just keeps playing."

**M-END-2: "The Silence"**
"You stopped listening. The decision was quieter than you expected — a release, not a sacrifice. The frequencies faded like a radio tuning to static and then past static to nothing.

The canal is just a canal. The church is just a church. The clock in the clockmaker's shop stopped again, and this time it stayed stopped.

But: the barista's song. That one melody, the 1967 song that was never recorded. You can still hum it. In the shower. Walking to the shops. It's the one thread that didn't break when you let go. One melody. In all of London's silence, one melody survived. Because you heard it. Because she hummed it. Because some sounds are stubborn enough to outlast the decision to stop listening."

**M-END-3: "The Instrument"**
"Your impression on the wall joins the others. Your ear-shape in the plaster. Your listening added to the archive. You didn't disappear — you're still here, still walking, still drinking coffee at the canal-side shop. But something of you is now in the walls. In the recording. Future Listeners will hear you alongside the 1967 song, alongside the medieval chant, alongside the river's two-thousand-year B-flat.

The Sound Artist recorded the moment you joined. She plays it back for you. You hear yourself listening. It sounds like breathing."

### Wanderer Endings (representative — others follow same depth)

**W-END-1: "The Waking"**
"Beneath the empty lot. Past the fox. Past the last of the streetlight. Down into a space that has no architecture, only feeling.

London's consciousness is not what you expected. Not a voice. Not a presence. A gravity. A warmth so vast it has weather. You stood in it the way you stand in a cathedral — small, held, overwhelmed by the scale of what contains you.

You woke it. Not with a word or an action. With attention. Yours was the attention that tipped the balance. London opened its eyes — not eyes, not really, but the best word you have — and the dream became the city and the city became the dream and there is no longer any difference between the London that sleeps and the London that walks above.

The foxes know. They always knew."

**W-END-2: "The Dream-Keeper"**
"You stepped back from the edge. Not out of fear — out of tenderness. The dream is beautiful. The dream contains everything London has ever felt and hasn't finished processing. If it ended — if London woke fully — all of that unprocessed feeling would flood the streets. Every grief, every joy, every ordinary Tuesday morning of every person who has ever lived here, all at once.

You chose to keep the dream alive. You walk the boundary now. Between sleeping and waking. Between the city people see and the city you know is there. You are the guardian of the threshold. The foxes follow you. The old man nods as you pass his bench each evening. The barista saves your seat.

You'll walk until it wakes. Or until you do. For now, London dreams. And you're the one who makes sure it dreams gently."
