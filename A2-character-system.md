# A2: Character System Document

## The Five Traits

Each trait is a life, not a class. Each implies a way of being in a city. Each determines what the player perceives, how they investigate, which mythology they experience, and how they relate to NPCs.

---

### The Musician
**Perception Mode:** Sound, conversation, auditory detail
**Verb:** Listens
**Starting Perception:** Hears ambient sounds more distinctly. Notices when something is musically wrong — a frequency that doesn't belong, a rhythm in footsteps that forms a pattern, silence where there should be sound.
**Mythological London:** The city is a recording. Every sound ever made still exists, layered and buried. The mythological layer is acoustic memory playing back.
**Investigation Style:** Talks to people. Listens between the lines. Follows auditory clues — a melody, a hum, a voice from the wrong century.
**Trait-Specific Stat: Resonance** — how clearly the hidden acoustic layer is perceived. At low Resonance: faint anomalies, easily dismissed. At high Resonance: full layered soundscapes, voices, songs, the city's acoustic architecture revealed.
**NPC Affinity:** The Sound Artist, the pub landlord (his warnings have rhythm), the canal boat painter (she hums while she works).
**District Affinity:** Limehouse.

---

### The Photographer
**Perception Mode:** Sight, pattern, visual detail
**Verb:** Sees
**Starting Perception:** Notices visual details others walk past. Light falling at wrong angles. Reflections that show something different from what's in front of the glass. Patterns in architecture that repeat across centuries.
**Mythological London:** The city is a palimpsest. Every version exists simultaneously — Roman, medieval, Tudor, Victorian, modern — written on the same page, older layers bleeding through.
**Investigation Style:** Examines, compares, maps. Photographs things and notices the photo shows something the eye didn't catch. Follows visual patterns across locations.
**Trait-Specific Stat: Clarity** — how sharply the palimpsest layers are perceived. At low Clarity: fleeting glimpses, tricks of light. At high Clarity: full visual overlays, past Londons visible alongside present.
**NPC Affinity:** The gallery owner (her questions are about what you see), the data scientist (patterns in data mirror patterns in light), the tattoo artist (his drawings show what's hidden).
**District Affinity:** Bermondsey (transformation visible in the material).

---

### The Wanderer
**Perception Mode:** Intuition, feeling, emotional charge
**Verb:** Feels
**Starting Perception:** Senses emotional charges in places. A pull toward certain streets. Déjà vu in locations never visited. An unexplained reluctance to enter certain buildings. The body knows something the mind doesn't.
**Mythological London:** The city is dreaming. The mythological layer is the dream. Certain places hold emotions London hasn't processed — trauma, joy, grief, wonder.
**Investigation Style:** Follows instinct. Takes the unexpected path. Trusts the feeling over the evidence. Goes where the pull leads and finds meaning in what seems random.
**Trait-Specific Stat: Attunement** — how strongly the dream's emotional currents are felt. At low Attunement: vague unease or warmth. At high Attunement: the dream becomes navigable, the player can feel their way through London's subconscious.
**NPC Affinity:** The nightclub promoter (she knows where the energy is), the urban explorer (he follows instinct into dark places), the old man on the bench (he drifts too).
**District Affinity:** Bermondsey (transformation is felt before it's seen).

---

### The Barista
**Perception Mode:** Connection, relationships, relational patterns
**Verb:** Connects
**Starting Perception:** Notices how people relate to each other. Who avoids whom. Who watches whom. Conversations overheard that seem unrelated but rhyme. The web of human connection that underlies a neighborhood.
**Mythological London:** The city is a network. Every person who has ever lived here is connected — through shared places, shared air, shared streets. The network is converging on something deliberately erased.
**Investigation Style:** Talks to everyone. Connects information from different NPCs. Notices when two people's stories share a detail neither mentioned. Maps relationships the way others map streets.
**Trait-Specific Stat: Threads** — how many connections can be perceived simultaneously. At low Threads: pairs, obvious links. At high Threads: vast webs spanning centuries, the hidden topology of London's human connections.
**NPC Affinity:** The market vendor (he knows everyone's price), the bike courier (he crosses all boundaries), the barista NPC in Limehouse (they recognize each other — same instinct).
**District Affinity:** Limehouse (the meeting point, the basin where paths converge).

---

### The Shopkeeper
**Perception Mode:** Permanence, memory, temporal depth
**Verb:** Remembers
**Starting Perception:** Notices what hasn't changed. The building that's the same as 1890. The NPC who doesn't age. The street that appears on no modern map but is still there. Sees the persistence beneath the flux.
**Mythological London:** London is built on something older than geology. The permanent things — stones that never move, ground that never floods — are anchored to a deep layer. Something alive. Something stirring.
**Investigation Style:** Compares then and now. Holds the city accountable to its own past. Researches, remembers, returns to places to see what's changed and what hasn't. The detective of continuity.
**Trait-Specific Stat: Depth** — how far back in time perception reaches. At low Depth: decades, living memory. At high Depth: centuries, millennia, the deep time of London's geological and pre-geological foundation.
**NPC Affinity:** The clockmaker (he keeps things that persist), the market vendor (antiques are persistence made portable), the observatory keeper (she measures time's passage).
**District Affinity:** Greenwich (the place that insists on measuring permanence).

## Core Stats (Universal)

### Awareness
**What it measures:** Perception depth — how much of London's hidden layer the player can access.
**Grows by:** Exploring new locations, noticing tappable details, discovering hidden content, finding lore fragments.
**What it unlocks:** New tappable details in previously visited locations (retroactive discovery). Hidden locations. Deeper NPC dialogue. The Watcher becomes visible at medium Awareness. Investigation-locked locations open at high Awareness.
**Visible expression:** The character's idle animation becomes more observant — head turns, eyes track, subtle shimmer around details they can perceive.

### Connection
**What it measures:** Relationship depth — how deeply bonded with NPCs and places.
**Grows by:** Repeated visits to the same NPC. Making choices that prioritize relationships over investigation. Returning to familiar locations. Sharing food. Sitting in silence with someone.
**What it unlocks:** NPC relationship stages advance. Deeper dialogue. NPC backstories. Personal quests. Mythological Ally status for select NPCs. The flat gains more personal objects.
**Visible expression:** NPCs approach the player instead of waiting. The character walks more confidently. The flat feels warmer (lighting shifts subtly).

### Insight
**What it measures:** Mythological understanding — how much the player grasps about what's happening to London.
**Grows by:** Finding lore fragments. Connecting contradictory clues. Making choices that demonstrate understanding. Reaching Confidant stage with deep NPCs. Completing investigation nodes.
**What it unlocks:** The deeper mythology. Ability to perceive during mythological weather shifts. Access to event endings. The final investigations in each neighborhood. The Watcher's full identity.
**Visible expression:** The character's shadow behaves slightly differently. Light bends around them. NPCs who are myth-aware react to the player with recognition.

## Progression Curve

**Sessions 1-5 (Arrival):** Everything is new. Low stats. The city is mostly ordinary. Occasional flickers of the mythological layer — easily dismissed. NPCs are polite but distant. The player is learning to notice.

**Sessions 6-15 (Settling):** Stats rising. A home neighborhood established. Several NPC relationships at Acquaintance or Familiar. The mythology is undeniable — too many anomalies to ignore. First investigation threads opening. The Watcher appears in the background.

**Sessions 16-30 (Belonging):** Significant stat growth. Deep NPC relationships. Multiple investigations active. The mythology is the player's reality. Trait-deepening reveals new layers in old locations. The flat is filling with objects. The Watcher confronts the player directly.

**Sessions 30+ (Depth):** High stats. Confidant and Mythological Ally NPCs. Main storyline approaching climax. Event endings becoming available. The player navigates London's mythological layer as fluently as the real one. The central question sharpens: what is London, and what should the player do about it?

**No hard gates on session count.** A player who plays intensively might reach Depth in 20 sessions. A casual player might take 50. The progression responds to engagement quality, not quantity.

## Character Creation Flow

1. **The flat.** The player sees their empty room. Pixel art. Rain outside. A mug on the table.
2. **The walk.** The player leaves the flat and walks through Limehouse. No choices yet — just experiencing the neighborhood. Three brief encounters: a sound (the canal), a sight (a reflection), a feeling (a pull toward a side street).
3. **The moment.** One of the three encounters resonates. The player taps it instinctively. The game notes which one.
4. **The choice.** The game presents five lives — not as a menu but as a scene. Five people in a café. A musician tuning a guitar. A photographer reviewing shots. Someone staring out the window (the wanderer). Someone talking to everyone (the barista). Someone examining the café's old fixtures (the shopkeeper). The player taps the person they identify with. That's their trait.
5. **The beginning.** The selected character becomes the player's avatar. The café scene fades. They're standing on Narrow Street. London is waiting. No tutorial. No explanation. Walk.

Total time: under 2 minutes. The player has made the most important choice in the game and it felt like a moment, not a menu.
