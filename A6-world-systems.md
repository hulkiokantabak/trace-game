## Metzen + Meier Design. Miyamoto Validates Feel. Ueda Validates Emotion.

## SEASONAL CYCLE

One real month per game season. Derived from real-world date.

### Spring (March — May)
**Palette:** Soft greens, warming light, gentle contrast. Morning light is the star.
**NPC shifts:** The Canal Boat Painter is outside more. The Park Astronomer appears. The market wakes earlier.
**Location changes:** Greenwich Park blooms. The Thames foreshore is more accessible (lower spring tides). The rooftop in Bermondsey becomes pleasant.
**Mythology:** The mythological layer is gentle — more suggestions than intrusions. London is stretching, waking gently. New details appear at familiar locations (spring growth as metaphor for retroactive discovery).
**Seasonal event pool:** The Greenwich meridian recalibration (annual event the Observatory Keeper mentions). Canal boat painting season begins. A new mural in Bermondsey.

### Summer (June — August)
**Palette:** Warm, bright, long golden evenings. High contrast. Strong shadows.
**NPC shifts:** Evening NPCs are more available (longer days). The Nightclub Promoter runs outdoor events. The Bike Courier works later.
**Location changes:** The rooftop is at its best. The canal basin is crowded. Greenwich Park is busy with picnickers (more ambient life). The warehouse at night is hot and strange.
**Mythology:** The Bright Tide is most likely in summer — London's dream at its most vivid, almost visible in daylight. The mythological layer is bold.
**Seasonal event pool:** A solstice event at the Observatory. A night market expansion in Limehouse. A summer opening at the gallery.

### Autumn (September — November)
**Palette:** Amber, rust, copper. Fog becomes common. The canal in autumn mist is the game's most atmospheric location.
**NPC shifts:** The Old Man stays longer (dusk comes earlier). The Sound Artist records more (autumn acoustics). The Data Scientist finds more anomalies (patterns intensify in autumn data).
**Location changes:** The park path is carpeted in leaves. The foreshore reveals different objects (autumn tides). The covered market gets cozy. The flat's window shows changing trees.
**Mythology:** The mythology is richest in autumn — layered, complex, the most content visible. The Restless Tide is most common. Lore fragments feel more urgent.
**Seasonal event pool:** The antiques market autumn special. An acoustic event in the railway arch. A Hawksmoor lecture at St Anne's.

### Winter (December — February)
**Palette:** Cold blues, greys, warm interiors glow. Snow is rare but transforms everything when it arrives.
**NPC shifts:** Outdoor NPCs move inside (Canal Boat Painter, Old Man appears less). Indoor NPCs are more available and more talkative (longer evenings in the pub, the coffee shop, the workshop). The Warehouse Guard's nights are longest.
**Location changes:** The foot tunnel is colder. The foreshore is less accessible (higher winter tides). The flat feels most like home — warm against the cold.
**Mythology:** The Still Tide is most common in winter. London sleeps more deeply. The Forgetting hits harder. But when the mythology does appear in winter, it's stark and undeniable — no soft edges, no gentle suggestions. Winter mythology is the bone underneath the skin.
**Seasonal event pool:** A winter market in Greenwich. New Year's on the Thames path. A candlelit evening at the old church.

## CITY EVENTS

One active at a time. Duration: 3-5 real days. New event every 2-3 weeks. Events are drawn from a rotation pool and seeded by date to ensure all players experience them simultaneously.

**Rotation Rule:** No more than 2 consecutive trait-specific events. Every third event must be universal (events 1-15 in the pool). This ensures all players, regardless of trait, experience regular world-level changes.

### Event Pool (20 events, expanding over time)

**Greenwich Events:**
1. **Observatory Anomaly** — Triggered by GI-03 if reported. Clock anomaly makes local news. Media at the observatory. The Observatory Keeper is stressed. The Watcher is more visible.
2. **Park After Hours** — Greenwich Park stays open past dark for one night (a real event London does occasionally). The park at night is mythologically vivid.
3. **Meridian Festival** — Tourists flood the meridian line. The player sees things in the crowd nobody else does.
4. **The Ship in the Dry Dock** — The Cutty Sark "behaves strangely" — rigging moves without wind, the hold sounds occupied. A one-night investigation window.

**Limehouse Events:**
5. **Basin Arrival** — A narrowboat arrives that wasn't expected. It has no registered owner. It's been on the canal network for eleven years without stopping. The Canal Boat Painter recognizes the paint.
6. **St Anne's Night** — The church opens for a night service for the first time in years. The interior is different at night (LI-04 triggers more easily).
7. **Sound Installation** — The Sound Artist gets permission for a public installation at the basin. She plays her recordings. The public hears ambiance. The Musician hears the hidden layer.
8. **The Night Market Expansion** — The market appears on a night it shouldn't — a Tuesday, or a Sunday. The Nightclub Promoter didn't organize it. Nobody did.

**Bermondsey Events:**
9. **New Mural** — A mural appears overnight on a railway arch. Nobody saw it painted. It depicts something mythologically significant — trait-dependent interpretation.
10. **Market Dawn** — The antiques market has a pre-dawn hour where different objects appear — objects from the foundation layer. Only early-arriving players find them.
11. **Gallery After Dark** — The gallery opens at midnight. By invitation. The Gallery Owner asks: "Ready?" The art is different at night.
12. **Warehouse Auction** — The warehouse's contents are supposedly being auctioned. The auction is real but the objects on offer don't match what's inside. The Night Guard says nothing. One word: "Wrong."

**Cross-Neighborhood Events:**
13. **The Fox Trail** — The Night Fox appears in all three neighborhoods on the same night, leaving a visible trail. Following it from Greenwich to Limehouse to Bermondsey reveals something at each stop.
14. **The Thames Surge** — An unusually high tide. The foreshore is submerged. But in Limehouse basin, the water is lower than expected. Something is pulling it.
15. **The Walking Figure** — The Thames Path Figure (BI-10) is seen by multiple NPCs across all neighborhoods. The Watcher is agitated. Something is moving through London that shouldn't be.
16. **All Clocks** — Every clock in Greenwich — the Observatory, the clockmaker's, the church — shows a different time on the same day. The times, when plotted, form a sequence.
17. **The Chalk Map** — The Child Who Draws has drawn a massive map overnight, covering an entire pavement section. The map shows all three neighborhoods. And a fourth.
18. **Network Pulse** — For Barista players: every NPC mentions the same word on the same day. Different contexts. Same word. The word is the first syllable of the erased name.
19. **Deep Tremor** — For Shopkeeper players: a tremor felt only in specific locations. Not seismic. Felt in the hands, not the feet. The foundation marks are warm.
20. **Signal Spike** — For Musician players: the canal frequency spikes for 24 hours. The Sound Artist records continuously. The recording, played at half speed, contains a word.

## MYTHOLOGICAL WEATHER

Four states. Cycle independently of real weather. Each state lasts 3-7 real days. The cycle is semi-random but weighted by season (see seasonal notes above).

### The Restless Tide
**Duration:** 3-5 days. **Frequency:** Most common in autumn.
**Visual effect:** Slight warmth added to palette. Pixel details shimmer faintly. More discoverable content visible.
**NPC effect:** NPCs say slightly stranger things. More mythological clues surface in dialogue. The Watcher is more active.
**Sound:** Ambient layer has an additional sub-frequency — not consciously audible but felt.
**Gameplay:** This is the "richest" state for investigation. More nodes are advanceable. More details are noticeable. The game is at its most generous.

### The Deep Tide
**Duration:** 4-7 days. **Frequency:** Common in winter and spring.
**Visual effect:** Palette slightly cooler, shadows deeper, more contrast. Interiors feel warmer.
**NPC effect:** NPCs are more contemplative. Conversations go deeper. Relationship progression is faster — NPCs share more readily.
**Sound:** Ambient layer is quieter. Fewer background sounds. Silence has more presence.
**Gameplay:** The best state for NPC relationship building. Less investigation content but deeper emotional content. The game turns inward.

### The Bright Tide
**Duration:** 1-2 days. **Frequency:** Rare. Most likely in summer.
**Visual effect:** The pixel art glows. Light sources are brighter. Colors are more saturated. The most visually beautiful state. Mythological details are almost visible to the naked eye — the palimpsest shimmers, the sound is almost audible, the emotional charges are almost tangible.
**NPC effect:** NPCs are awed, unsettled, or joyful — depending on character. The Watcher is absent (hiding, or overwhelmed).
**Sound:** A faint, ambient chord. Not music. A resonance. The city humming.
**Gameplay:** Rare trait-gated content is only available during Bright Tides. The title "Dream-Touched" requires experiencing a Bright Tide at high Awareness. This is the game's peak state — fleeting and unmissable.

### The Still Tide
**Duration:** 1-3 days. **Frequency:** Precedes The Forgetting. Most common in winter.
**Visual effect:** Palette desaturated. Details are less sharp. The pixel art feels like a photograph slightly out of focus.
**NPC effect:** NPCs are quiet. They speak less. The ones who know something is coming (the Clockmaker, the Warehouse Guard, the Pub Landlord) show it in their bodies — the Clockmaker's hands slow down, the Guard pauses longer between patrol rounds.
**Sound:** One frequency is missing from the ambient layer. The player won't consciously notice which. Their body will.
**Gameplay:** Investigation is harder — fewer details visible, fewer clues available. But: Insight XP is earned during The Still Tide by simply being present. The game rewards the player for staying in the quiet.

## THE FORGETTING

**Frequency:** Approximately once per week. Unpredictable within a 5-9 day window after the previous one ended.
**Duration:** 1-2 real days.
**Preceded by:** The Still Tide (always).

**Visual effect:** Full desaturation is not applied (this isn't the tending game). Instead: one color is muted. Not removed — reduced. The player won't know which color. Autumn reds become browns. Summer greens lose vibrancy. The effect is subliminal.

**NPC effect:** NPCs at Stranger/Acquaintance: no visible change. NPCs at Familiar: slightly more distant, slightly uncertain. They remember you but like a dream. "You seem... familiar. Sorry. Long day." NPCs at Confidant+: affected but resistant. They know something is wrong. "The clocks all slowed. They do that sometimes." They're shaken but they hold.

**Mythological effect:** The mythological layer goes quiet. Trait-gated details are invisible. Lore fragments are not discoverable. Investigation nodes cannot be advanced. The city is just a city. The player who plays during The Forgetting experiences London as ordinary — and the ordinariness, after weeks of strangeness, is eerie.

**Sound:** The ambient sub-frequency (present during Restless and Deep tides) is absent. Complete baseline ambiance. No additional layer. The silence where the mythology used to be.

**Gameplay value:** During The Forgetting, high-Insight players can notice things invisible during vivid states. The absence reveals. What doesn't disappear during The Forgetting is the most permanent, most deeply anchored content — the foundation marks (Shopkeeper), the deepest sounds (Musician), the most persistent visual anomalies (Photographer). The Forgetting is a filter: what passes through it is the most real.

**Recovery:** The Forgetting lifts gradually over 6-12 hours. The missing color returns. NPCs resume their normal behavior (often with a comment — "Strange couple of days"). The mythological layer returns, slightly more vivid than before The Forgetting, as if the rest refreshed it.

## AGGREGATE MULTIPLAYER SYSTEMS

### District Warmth
**Calculation:** Sum of all player and agent visits to a district's locations in the past 7 days. Stored as integer per district.
**Thresholds:** Cold (<20), Mild (20-50), Warm (51-100), Vibrant (100+).
**Effects:** Warm/Vibrant: slightly more vivid palette, NPCs have additional ambient lines, more ambient life sprites, more discoverable details. Cold: slightly muted palette, NPCs more reserved, fewer ambient sprites.
**Update frequency:** Recalculated daily.

### The Dreaming Index
**Calculation:** Total player + agent activity across all districts in past 7 days.
**Effect:** Modifies the intensity of the mythological weather. At high Dreaming Index, Restless Tides are more vivid and Bright Tides more likely. At low Dreaming Index, Still Tides are more common and The Forgetting hits harder.
**Update:** Daily.

### Residuum Marks
**Placement:** At key investigation locations and significant personal locations.
**Data:** Counter per trait per location. `{ musician: 3, photographer: 1, ... }`
**Display:** Small symbols visible at the location. The player sees the count but not who left them. "Three Musician marks on this bench."
**Effect:** High mark counts make a location slightly warmer (independent of district warmth). A location loved by many players persists — it's the game's version of collective care.

### World Milestones
**Tracked:** Total marks placed, total players who've reached Confidant with an NPC, total investigations resolved, total agents created.
**Effects:** At specific thresholds, small world changes occur — a new ambient detail, a new shallow NPC appearance, a new found journal page. These changes are not announced. They simply appear. Players who notice them recognize: the world grew.
