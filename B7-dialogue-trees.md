# B7: DIALOGUE TREES

## Vian + Saramago Lead. All Conversations Follow Voice Rules (B3).

### Principles

Every dialogue tree has two layers: **neutral** (all traits see the same text) and **trait-gated** (specific lines visible only to a specific trait). Neutral dialogue covers relationship progression. Trait dialogue carries mythological content.

**DIALOGUE LIMITS (amended post-editorial):**
- Standard dialogue: ≤12 words
- Confidant reveals: ≤15 words (these moments are earned and need room to breathe)
- One seismic moment per NPC: ≤20 words (a full sentence from the Warehouse Guard, the Old Man's complete thought)
- When an NPC speaks longer than usual, the player knows something has shifted. Length IS the signal.

The tree is not exhaustive — it covers scripted beats. Between beats, the NPC is in "open mode" (ambient line in scripted play, live conversation in AI mode).

**Ambient lines** are what the NPC says when no scripted beat is active. 2-3 per NPC per stage, rotating. These are the texture of returning — the small acknowledgments that make the NPC feel alive.

## GREENWICH NPCs

### 1. THE CLOCKMAKER

**Stage 1: Stranger**
- Entry: "We close at five." [neutral]
- Ambient: "Don't touch the regulators." / "Parts are in the back." [neutral]

**Stage 2: Acquaintance**
- Trigger line: "You again. The spring you asked about. Still thinking." [neutral]
- Ambient: "Quiet morning. Good for the mainsprings." / "Rain makes the wood swell. The clocks speed up." [neutral]
- [musician]: "You hear that? The number seven case. It resonates."
- [photographer]: "You keep looking at the back wall. The light does something there."
- [wanderer]: "You always pause at the same spot. Interesting."
- [barista]: "The woman from the observatory hasn't been in. Used to come Tuesdays."
- [shopkeeper]: "That case is older than the glass. Older than the counter."

**Stage 3: Familiar**
- Trigger line: "Sit. I made too much tea." [neutral]
- Key beat — the stopped clock: "That one keeps its own time. Always has." [neutral]
- Post-investigation: "You wound it." / "You left it. Smart." [neutral, branches per GI-01 choice]
- [musician]: "Listen. When it's quiet. The shop has a pitch."
- [photographer]: "I found something. Under the face of the number twelve. Another face."
- [wanderer]: "Some mornings the shop feels different. Heavier. I make tea and wait."
- [barista]: "She sent a letter. I didn't open it. It's in the drawer."
- [shopkeeper]: "The floor creaks there because something's underneath. I've never looked."

**Stage 4: Confidant**
- Trigger line [neutral]: "I've been here forty years. The shop's been here two hundred and forty."
- [musician]: "The oldest clock isn't keeping time. It's keeping pitch."
- [photographer]: "Every clock here has had another face. I keep the old faces in a drawer. Want to see?"
- [wanderer]: "I tried to retire once. Got to Paddington. My feet wouldn't go."
- [barista]: "She and I measured the same thing differently. She used numbers. I used hands. We stopped agreeing."
- [shopkeeper]: "The shop was here before the street had a name. I'm keeping it going until the street remembers."

**Stage 5: Mythological Ally** (trait-specific, only reached by 1-2 traits per playthrough)
- [musician]: "The pitch is rising. Hear it? The whole city is tuning. Something's about to play."
- [shopkeeper]: "Come. I'll show you the floor. What's underneath. Bring the key if you have it."

### 21. The Night Fox
No dialogue. Behavioral communication:
- First encounter: Eye contact. Holds for 3 seconds. Turns and walks. If followed: leads to a hidden detail at the current location.
- Subsequent: Appears at different locations. Sometimes waits. Sometimes leads. Sometimes shakes its head and leaves. The head-shake means: not this way. Not yet.
- At the empty lot (L10): Sits at the entrance. Doesn't leave. Guards the space. If the player has sufficient Awareness, the fox steps aside.

### 22. The Child Who Draws
- Every encounter: "Not yet." [neutral] Spoken without looking up.
- At high Insight (Tier 3): The child looks up for the first time. Says one line. Trait-specific:
  - [musician]: "It's almost loud enough."
  - [photographer]: "The next page is blank."
  - [wanderer]: "It's almost awake."
  - [barista]: "I almost remember the name."
  - [shopkeeper]: "The ground is warm today."
Then returns to drawing. Never speaks again.
