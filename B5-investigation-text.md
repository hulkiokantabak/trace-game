# B5: INVESTIGATION TEXT

## Cortázar + Vian Lead. Saramago Grounds in Body. Borges Checks Architecture.

### Principles

Investigation text follows the game's economy rules: clues are short (≤12 words), choices are clear and genuinely different, consequences are felt not explained. The player is an accomplice — they construct meaning from evidence, never from narration.

All investigation text is tagged: `neutral` (all traits see) or trait-specific (`musician`, `photographer`, `wanderer`, `barista`, `shopkeeper`).

## GREENWICH INVESTIGATION TEXT

### GI-01: The Stopped Clock

**Discovery text** [neutral]: "One clock on the back wall isn't moving. Every other one is."

**Clue progression:**
- Visit 2 [neutral]: "The stopped clock's hands have moved. Not correctly. Tracking something else."
- Visit 3 [musician]: "The clock ticks when you lean close. Not ticking. Vibrating. At a pitch."
- Visit 3 [photographer]: "The clock's face reflects a room. Not this room. Deeper. Older panelling."
- Visit 3 [wanderer]: "Standing near it. Your sense of time thickens. The moment stretches."
- Visit 3 [barista]: "The clockmaker tensed when you looked at it. The same way the observatory keeper tensed last week."
- Visit 3 [shopkeeper]: "The wood of the casing is older than anything else in the shop. Older than the building."

**Choice text** [neutral]: 
The clockmaker holds a key. Small, brass, worn. He looks at the stopped clock.
- **[Wind it]** "Let's see what it does."
- **[Don't]** "Some things are stopped for a reason."

**Consequence — wind** [neutral]: "The clock resumes. A different sound fills the shop — not ticking. Ringing. The clockmaker closes his eyes. 'There it is,' he says. Outside, the glass in the case unclouds. The street sounds have changed pitch. The clockmaker's hands, for the first time, are still."

**Consequence — don't** [neutral]: "'Smart,' the clockmaker says. He puts the key back. His hands are shaking. 'I've never wound it either.' He pours tea. The silence between you is different now. Warmer."

### GI-03: The Meridian Deviation

**Discovery text** [neutral]: Observatory Keeper: "Eleven seconds. Since Tuesday."

**Clue progression:**
- Research [neutral]: "The bookshop has a record. The meridian shifted in 1894. In 1666. In 1348. Each time: crisis."
- [musician]: "The canal's note shifted on the same Tuesday. Same magnitude. Different medium."
- [photographer]: "Photographs from 1894 show the brass line in a position three centimetres from where it is now."
- [wanderer]: "The observatory terrace. The air is tense. London is anxious. Something is being measured and London knows."
- [barista]: "The clockmaker sent the observatory keeper a letter that week. She didn't open it. It's in her desk."
- [shopkeeper]: "The ground beneath the observatory: settled chalk over London clay. The chalk is the oldest stable layer in Greenwich. It moved."

**Choice text** [neutral]:
The Observatory Keeper: "I need to re-measure. At night. The instruments are more accurate without foot traffic. Will you help?"
- **[Stay the night]** "I'll be there."
- **[Decline]** "I'm not sure I should."

**Consequence — stay** [neutral]: "The night at the observatory. The instruments. The quiet. And at 3:17 AM, something the instruments recorded that the Observatory Keeper has never seen. She prints it. Her hands are steady but her voice is not. 'Do we report this?' she asks."
- **[Report]**: "The anomaly enters the public record. Within a week, a journalist calls the observatory. A city event triggers. The Watcher appears in Greenwich for the first time."
- **[Keep private]**: "The printout goes in her desk. Next to the clockmaker's unopened letter. 'Thank you,' she says. 'For staying. And for understanding.' She advances to Confidant."

### GI-05: The Ghost Pub

**Discovery text** [neutral]: Pub Landlord at Familiar stage: "There used to be a pub on Crane Street. Demolished in '87. It's still there. On Thursdays."

**Clue** [neutral]: "Thursday evening. Crane Street. Where the old pub was. A warm light, faint, where no building stands."

**Entry text** [neutral]: "You step toward the glow. The air changes — warmer, closer, thick with hops and conversation. The light solidifies around you. Floorboards underfoot. A bar. Patrons. A pub that doesn't exist, full of people who don't notice they're impossible. One approaches. 'What'll you have?'"

**The drink** [trait-specific]:
- [musician]: "It tastes like a song you knew as a child but never heard."
- [photographer]: "It tastes like the color of the light through your grandmother's curtains."
- [wanderer]: "It tastes like home. Not any home you've had. The home you've been looking for."
- [barista]: "It tastes like the first conversation that mattered."
- [shopkeeper]: "It tastes like something that hasn't changed. Like the flavour of before."

**Choice text** [neutral]:
- **[Tell the Pub Landlord]** "I found it. The pub on Crane Street."
- **[Keep the secret]** "This is mine."

## LIMEHOUSE INVESTIGATION TEXT

### LI-01: The Unrecorded Sound

**Discovery** [musician]: Sound Artist: "Listen to the background." She plays the canal recording. Behind water, behind wind. A note. Sustained. Impossible.

**Clue progression:**
- [musician]: "47 recordings. Different locations. Different times. The same note. Always there."
- [musician]: "Mapped: the note follows a line. The old course of the Limehouse Cut before 1770."
- [neutral]: "The line passes through buildings. Through walls. One building: the note is loudest."
- [neutral]: "The building is sealed. No entrance. The Urban Explorer: 'I know a way.'"

**Choice text** [neutral]:
- **[Enter with the Explorer]** "Show me."
- **[Analyse with the Data Scientist]** "There might be another way to understand it."

**Consequence — enter** [musician]: "Inside. The walls are covered in impressions. Handprints. Ear shapes. Pressed into the surface like fossils. People have been here before. Listening. The note is overwhelming. You press your ear to the wall. The wall is warm. Your impression joins the others."

**Consequence — analyse** [musician]: "The Data Scientist's graph: the note's volume over time. Exponential. Increasing. She looks up. 'It correlates with something.' She pulls another dataset. Active listeners. People who hear things. The more who listen, the louder it gets. 'You're amplifying it,' she says. 'By hearing it.'"

### LI-02: The Barista's Song

**Discovery** [neutral]: "The barista hums while she works. A melody. Specific. You can't place it."

**Clue progression:**
- [neutral]: "The melody is from 1967. Never recorded. The band broke up before they finished it."
- [neutral]: "The song was written in this building. When it was a recording studio."
- [neutral]: "The songwriter lived upstairs. The flat above the café. Your flat."

**Choice text** [neutral]:
You know the song's origin. The barista is humming a song written in your flat by a person who died before she was born.
- **[Tell her]** "That song. I know where it comes from."
- **[Say nothing]** Let her hum.

**Consequence — tell** [neutral]: "She stops. 'What do you mean?' You tell her. She tries to hum it again. She can't. The melody is gone. 'I don't... I can't remember how it goes.' She looks at her hands. 'But I can tell you something about your flat. About what I hear through the floor at night.'"

**Consequence — say nothing** [neutral]: "She hums. You listen. The melody fills the café the way it has every morning. Some knowledge is better as a secret. The mystery deepens. The music stays."

## BERMONDSEY INVESTIGATION TEXT

### BI-01: The Antiques Market Object

**Discovery** [neutral]: "A key. Brass. The metal is wrong — not old enough for antique, not new enough for modern. The Market Vendor: 'It's been here. It's always been here.'"

**Clue progression:**
- [neutral]: "Metal analysis (Data Scientist): unknown alloy. Mineral traces found only in Thames Basin chalk deposits."
- [neutral]: "The key has a maker's mark. A symbol. The same symbol appears on the oldest map of Bermondsey (bookshop, Greenwich)."
- [shopkeeper]: "Foundation marks. Seven across London. The symbol marks where London is anchored to what's beneath."
- [neutral]: "You find the seven marks. They form a geometry. The center: beneath St Mary Magdalen, Bermondsey."

**Choice text** [neutral]:
The church. The crypt door. The key fits.
- **[Turn the key]** "It's been waiting."
- **[Return the key to the Market Vendor]** "Some doors should stay closed."

**Consequence — turn** [shopkeeper]: "The door opens onto stairs that go deeper than the crypt should allow. Past Victorian brick. Past medieval stone. Past Roman concrete. Into something older. At the bottom: a space. Not carved. Grown. The walls are warm. A stone in the center of the floor hums. The oldest thing in London. You touch it."

**Consequence — return** [neutral]: "The Market Vendor takes the key. Wraps it in cloth. Places it under the counter. 'It'll wait,' she says. 'It's been waiting.' She looks at you. 'You're the first to bring it back.' Her trust deepens to something beyond Confidant. She tells you what she knows about the ground beneath Bermondsey. Everything she knows."

### BI-03: The Warehouse at Night

**Discovery** [neutral]: Warehouse Guard: "Careful." Then, after a silence: "It moves."

**Entry text** [neutral]: "The warehouse at night. Your footsteps echo in a space larger than the building should contain. The guard's torch makes pools of light in a darkness that feels attentive."

**Clue progression:**
- [neutral]: "Return visit. The shelving has rearranged. The door that was left is now right."
- [neutral]: "The guard's notebook: forty years of configurations. The warehouse shifts. It follows a pattern."
- [neutral]: "The pattern is cyclical. The configurations are converging on a specific arrangement."
- [neutral]: "The arrangement forms a door. Not a doorway. The space itself becomes a door."

**Choice text** [neutral]:
The guard: "It moves fastest at night. If you stay, you'll feel it. I stay every night. I always feel it."
- **[Stay the night]** "I'll stay."
- **[Study the maps]** "Show me the notebooks instead."

**Consequence — stay** [neutral]: "The night. The darkness. The slow, impossible rearrangement of space around you. Time dilates. What felt like an hour was six. The guard finds you at dawn. 'You look different,' he says. One word. 'Different.' Your Insight has jumped. Your sprite has changed. Your notebook contains an entry you don't remember writing."

## CROSS-NEIGHBORHOOD INVESTIGATION TEXT

### XN-03: The Watcher's Confrontation

**Discovery** [neutral]: The Watcher is sitting on the bench where the Old Man usually sits. The Old Man is absent. The Watcher: "I sat where you're sitting. Three years ago."

**Dialogue** [trait-specific]:
- [musician]: "I heard everything. For three years. Then it started hearing me back. The archive doesn't just play. It records. Every listener becomes a track. You're being recorded now."
- [photographer]: "I saw the next draft. London's revision. It's beautiful. It's perfect. There are no people in it. The city is editing us out."
- [wanderer]: "I followed the pull to its source. I found what's dreaming. It's not what you think. It's not kind. It's not unkind. It's vast. It noticed me the way you notice an ant. And then it didn't look away."
- [barista]: "I erased them. I had to. They were becoming the network. Every connection in London flowing through one mind. I broke the web to save it. I broke someone to save everyone. And the web is healing. You're healing it. Stop."
- [shopkeeper]: "I touched the ground. The real ground. Beneath the clay. It moved. Not like an earthquake. Like a breath. I've been monitoring it for seven years. It's breathing faster."

**Choice text** [neutral]:
The Watcher: "I'm not warning you. I'm telling you what happens."
- **[Continue]** "I need to see for myself."
- **[Stop]** "Maybe you're right."

**Consequence — continue** [neutral]: "The Watcher stands. 'Then you'll see.' They walk away. They don't look back. Your Awareness spikes. The mythological layer becomes vivid — more vivid than it's ever been. The city is bright and strange and alive in ways that are not entirely comforting. There's no going back. The Watcher is gone. You're the furthest anyone has been."

**Consequence — stop** [neutral]: "The Watcher looks at you for a long time. 'Thank you,' they say. They leave something on the bench — a small object, trait-dependent. You pick it up. The mythological layer dims. Not gone — diminished. London is quieter. The canal is just a canal. The clock is just a clock. But sometimes, at dusk, at the edge of perception, you still see it. You'll always see it. You just chose not to look."
