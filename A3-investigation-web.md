# A3: INVESTIGATION WEB

## Meier Architects. Metzen Builds Narrative.

### Overview

35 investigation nodes: 10 per neighborhood + 3 cross-neighborhood + 2 ambient (repeatable). Node types: Quick (1-2 sessions), Deep (3-6 sessions), Climactic (endgame, contains event ending choice).

---

## GREENWICH (10 Nodes)

### GI-01: The Stopped Clock (Quick → Deep)
**Location:** G01. **Access:** Universal entry, trait-specific depth.
One clock in the shop is stopped. All others tick. The clockmaker: "That one keeps its own time."
- Return visits: the stopped clock has moved, tracking something other than hours
- Musician: it ticks at a frequency, tuning to something beneath the canal
- Photographer: its face reflects a room that isn't behind it
- Wanderer: standing near it warps the sense of time passing
- Barista: the clockmaker and observatory keeper both avoid discussing it — connected silence
- Shopkeeper: it predates the shop, the building, the street
**Choice:** The clockmaker offers to let you wind it. Accept (something in Greenwich shifts, GI-03 opens) or decline (he trusts your caution, relationship deepens, thread closes gracefully).

### GI-02: The Tunnel That Stretches (Quick)
**Location:** G04. **Access:** Universal.
The foot tunnel takes longer than it should. Tile count changes by six or seven between visits. CCTV Operator note found behind a tile: "Camera 12, 02:47, tunnel measured 371m. Spec: 370.2m." No resolution — an ambient fact. Insight XP.

### GI-03: The Meridian Deviation (Deep)
**Location:** G02, links to G01. **Trigger:** GI-01 resolved OR Observatory Keeper at Familiar.
The meridian has shifted eleven seconds. Research reveals: it shifted before in 1894, 1666, 1348 — each a London crisis. Each trait interprets differently. **Choice:** Help re-measure (spend a night at the observatory). Report findings officially (city event triggers, Watcher more active) or keep private (Observatory Keeper advances to Confidant).

### GI-04: The Foreshore Objects (Quick, repeatable)
**Location:** G09. **Access:** Universal, objects trait-specific.
Low tide reveals objects: a humming clay pipe (Musician), glass that shows another century's opposite bank (Photographer), a warm stone (Wanderer), a button matching a Bermondsey NPC's (Barista), a coin from no known mint (Shopkeeper). Objects cycle per tide. Each connects to a lore fragment. Flat objects.

### GI-05: The Ghost Pub (Deep)
**Location:** G10 + unlisted. **Trigger:** Pub Landlord at Familiar: "It's still there. On Thursdays."
A demolished pub glows faintly on Thursday evenings. Inside: a warm space, NPCs from no other part of the game. One drink that tastes like a trait-specific memory. **Choice:** Tell the Pub Landlord (he already knows — relationship deepens, mythological connection revealed) or keep it secret (the ghost pub becomes a private retreat, a pocket of permanence).

### GI-06: The Park Astronomer (Deep)
**Location:** G08. **Access:** Photographer/Shopkeeper primary.
A figure in Greenwich Park at night with a telescope pointed at the ground. They're mapping something beneath the park. Investigation reveals: a survey of the foundation. The park sits on one of the oldest settled points in London. What's beneath it is moving — slowly, but measurably.

### GI-07: The Painted Hall Whispers (Quick)
**Location:** G06. **Access:** Musician primary.
Voices from the painted ceiling of the Naval College. Not ghosts — the painted figures are arguing. About something that hasn't happened yet. The argument concerns a decision the player will face later.

### GI-08: The Market Stall That Doesn't Pack Up (Quick)
**Location:** G05. **Access:** Universal.
One stall never closes. The vendor changes daily but the table remains. Contents shift by trait: sheet music (Musician), old photographs (Photographer), compass and map (Wanderer), letters between strangers (Barista), a stone that doesn't weather (Shopkeeper).

### GI-09: The Church Register (Deep)
**Location:** G03/G07. **Access:** Shopkeeper primary, all traits.
St Alfege's register: one name appears every century, different handwriting. The name connects to the Barista's erased person mythology and the Shopkeeper's foundation mythology simultaneously — a shared node with different interpretations.

### GI-10: The Old Man's Complete Sentence (Deep)
**Trigger:** Old Man at Confidant.
He speaks a complete sentence for the first and only time. Trait-dependent:
- Musician: "The river has been singing the same note since before the bridge."
- Photographer: "I watched the church rebuild itself one night. Stone by stone."
- Wanderer: "The bench knows when I'm coming. It gets warm."
- Barista: "Everyone on this street knew her name once. Now nobody does."
- Shopkeeper: "The ground here hasn't moved in eight hundred years. Until last week."
This sentence unlocks the Greenwich thread of the player's main storyline.

## LIMEHOUSE (12 Nodes)

### LI-01: The Unrecorded Sound (Deep — Musician main entry)
**Location:** L04. Sound Artist plays a canal recording. Behind water and wind: a sustained impossible note.
47 recordings share the anomaly. Mapped: the note follows the old canal route pre-1770. One building on the route is sealed. **Choice:** Enter with Urban Explorer (direct experience — the walls bear impressions of previous listeners) or analyse with Data Scientist (the note's volume correlates with the number of active Listeners in the city).

### LI-02: The Barista's Song (Deep)
**Location:** L02. The Barista hums a 1967 melody never recorded. The song was written in this building when it was a studio. The player's flat was the songwriter's flat.
**Choice:** Tell her the origin (the humming stops — she can't remember once she knows — but she tells you something about the flat) or let her hum (the mystery deepens, her mythological identity strengthens).

### LI-03: The Tattoo That Leads (Deep)
**Location:** L06. The Tattoo Artist draws a door. Its components are scattered: a doorframe in Bermondsey, a handle in Greenwich, hinges in Limehouse, a keyhole in a sealed tunnel.
**Choice:** Assemble knowledge (Tattoo Artist draws the complete door, unlocking a later location) or search for the physical door (found in the empty lot, L10).

### LI-04: St Anne's After Dark (Deep)
**Location:** L03. Night visit: the church casts a shadow with more sides than it has. Real Hawksmoor mythology embedded as lore. The shadow moves at a rate inconsistent with the sun.
**Choice:** Enter at night (impossible interior, the Musician hears the building breathe) or study from outside (the shadow reveals a pattern matching the Child's maps).

### LI-05: The Lock Gate Mechanism (Quick)
**Location:** L07. Victorian gears turn without water pressure. Shopkeeper/Musician primary. The mechanism connects to something beneath the canal — the same layer the Shopkeeper's foundation mythology describes.

### LI-06: The Promoter's Hidden Event (Deep)
**Location:** L09 → hidden venue. The Nightclub Promoter invites you to a gathering of people who all perceive the mythological layer. They disagree about what they see — mirroring the five mythologies. The player realizes they're not unique. But nobody agrees on what London is. The event is the game's most direct meta-commentary.

### LI-07: The Courier's Warning (Quick)
The Bike Courier delivers a fragment: a specific location, a specific day, a specific warning. If the player goes: they witness something — trait-dependent. If they don't: the location is subtly different afterward. Consequence either way.

### LI-08: The DLR Anomaly (Quick)
**Location:** L08. The departure board shows stations that don't exist. "Next train: The Meridian. 3 min." Nobody else notices. The stations correspond to mythological locations. The train, if waited for, never arrives — but the platform gets colder.

### LI-09: The Canal Boat Logbook (Deep — Barista path)
**Location:** L01. The Canal Boat Painter's narrowboat has a previous owner's logbook. The journey begins on the Regent's Canal and ends somewhere the canal doesn't go. The previous owner connects to NPCs in Greenwich and Bermondsey — the Barista traces the web.

### LI-10: The Empty Lot Entity (Deep)
**Location:** L10. At high Awareness: the lot is not empty. A presence. Not hostile — curious. The Night Fox guards the entrance. The space has opinions about who enters. The Wanderer feels it strongest. The lot is the geographic center of the Wanderer's dream cartography.

### LI-11: The Sound Artist's Archive (Deep — Musician climactic path)
**Location:** L04. The complete archive, mapped chronologically, reveals: the anomalous sounds are increasing in complexity. They're not noise. They're a language. London is trying to say something. The Musician is the only one who can hear the full vocabulary.

### LI-12: The Watcher's First Appearance (Deep)
**Trigger:** Awareness Tier 2. The same figure appears in the background of three different location scenes. Same clothes. The Bike Courier has seen them. The Nightclub Promoter knows their name but won't say it. The investigation: who is the Watcher? The Watcher doesn't hide once noticed. They wait.

## BERMONDSEY (10 Nodes)

### BI-01: The Antiques Market Object (Deep — Shopkeeper main entry)
**Location:** B01. The key: unknown alloy, foundation mark matching oldest Bermondsey map. Seven marks across London form a geometry. The key fits at the center — beneath St Mary Magdalen.
**Choice:** Use the key (enter what's beneath — the oldest thing in London) or return it (Market Vendor's deepest trust, investigation pauses but doesn't end).

### BI-02: The Gallery's Rotating Exhibition (Deep, seasonal)
**Location:** B02. Each season: one anomalous piece, no provenance, responds to mythological weather. Four pieces over a year form a composite image — trait-dependent revelation. The Gallery Owner: "Interesting piece?" She knows more than she asks.

### BI-03: The Warehouse at Night (Deep — Awareness Tier 2)
**Location:** B04. Night Guard: "Careful." Then: "It moves."
The interior is larger than the exterior. Objects shift between visits. The guard's decades of notebooks map every configuration. The configurations cycle toward a specific arrangement: a door.
**Choice:** Spend the night (time dilation, direct transformation, Insight jump) or study the maps (understand intellectually, discover the door the Tattoo Artist drew).

### BI-04: The Railway Arch Renovation (Deep)
**Location:** B03. Tools move overnight. Materials arrive from nowhere. A blueprint emerges: the arch is becoming a resonance chamber. Nobody is building it. Or: something is building it through the hands of whoever enters.

### BI-05: The Bermondsey Street Palimpsest (Deep — Photographer path)
**Location:** Bermondsey Street surface. After rain: text appears beneath the pavement. Older names. Older numbers. Older messages. Each rain reveals a new portion. Six rains to read the complete letter. The letter is addressed to the city.

### BI-06: The Rooftop Revelation (Deep)
**Location:** B08. **Trigger:** Confidant with Urban Explorer or Nightclub Promoter.
From the rooftop: all three neighborhoods visible simultaneously. At high Awareness: a pattern in the streets. The layout forms a shape — trait-dependent. The Watcher has been here. A folding chair, faded from weather, still unfolded. They sat here and saw what the player now sees.

### BI-07: The Vinyl That Shouldn't Exist (Quick — Musician path)
**Location:** B10. A record from a nonexistent band, recorded at a studio that burned before it was built. The record contains the canal frequency from LI-01. A physical artifact of the acoustic mythology.

### BI-08: The Street Preacher's Prophecy (Quick)
**Location:** B09. After three encounters, the Preacher says something specific to your investigation — knowledge he shouldn't have. Following him: he turns a corner and is gone. Where he stood: text on the pavement. Not chalk. Something older.

### BI-09: The Child's Map (Deep — Barista path)
**Location:** Variable (the Child moves). An accurate map with one nonexistent building. The address receives mail. Investigating: someone lives at an address without a building. The erased person? A projection of the network? The Child draws the negative space the Barista can feel.

### BI-10: The Thames Path Figure (Deep)
**Location:** B05. A figure walks the path at dawn. Every day. Same direction. The Watcher is afraid of them. This figure predates the Watcher — predates the game's mythology. They are the oldest thing that moves in London. Following them leads beyond the map's edge.

## CROSS-NEIGHBORHOOD (3 Nodes)

### XN-01: The Network Convergence (Barista Climactic)
**Trigger:** Barista at high Threads, Confidant with 3+ NPCs across neighborhoods.
Seven NPCs are connected by knowledge they shouldn't share. They orbit an absence. The Barista reconstructs the erased person from negative space — what everyone remembers but can't name.
**Event Ending Choice:** Speak the name (the network vibrates, NPCs react, the Watcher is devastated) or keep it (the knowledge is carried privately, the hole remains, the Watcher's erasure stands).

### XN-02: The Dream Cartography (Wanderer Climactic)
**Trigger:** Wanderer at high Attunement, Confidant with 2+, all neighborhood threads advanced.
The emotional geography map is complete. London's dream-mind has a structure with a center beneath the empty lot. Going there: entering the dream directly.
**Event Ending Choice:** Enter (encounter London's consciousness) or step back (become the dream's guardian).

### XN-03: The Watcher's Confrontation (All Traits Climactic)
**Trigger:** Awareness Tier 3, Insight Tier 2, Watcher at Confidant.
**Location:** The Watcher chooses — one of three: the St Alfege bench (G03) where the Old Man usually sits, the Thames Path bench (B05) facing the river, or the empty lot (L10) where everything converges. The location reflects the Watcher's mood and the player's trait.
The Watcher reveals their full trait-dependent story. They ask the player to stop. They show evidence. They may be right.
**Event Ending Choice:** Continue (go further than anyone has, the Watcher withdraws) or stop (perception dims, mythology fades, London becomes ordinary — but you remember).

## ADDITIONAL INVESTIGATION HOOKS (Dead-End Resolution)

Two locations had no investigation content. Fixed:

### LI-13: The Grapes' Cellar (Quick)
**Location:** L05. **Trigger:** Pub Landlord at Acquaintance. He mentions the cellar in passing.
The cellar beneath The Grapes predates the pub by two centuries. At high Awareness, the Musician hears an amplification effect — the cellar resonates. The Shopkeeper notices the foundation stones don't match the building above. A lore-fragment discovery location and an ambient NPC conversation point. Connects to GI-05 (Ghost Pub) and M-F2 (the 1967 session tapes are "in the pub's walls").

### BI-11: The Researcher's Model (Quick)
**Location:** B06. **Trigger:** Visit B06 during weekday hours. Encounter the AI Researcher.
After his third analogy ("Imagine something that learns from being watched"), the player can examine his laptop screen when he steps away for coffee. The screen shows a simulation. The simulation looks like the game's own map. The player's location is marked. This is the game's most uncomfortable fourth-wall moment — is the AI Researcher modelling the game itself? No resolution. Insight XP. The player carries the discomfort.

## AMBIENT LOCATIONS

G05 (Covered Market) and G08 (Park Path) serve primarily as ambient/seasonal locations with rotating content rather than fixed investigation nodes. They are NOT dead ends — they offer:
- Seasonal events (G05: winter market, G08: spring blooms)
- Shallow NPC encounters (rotating market vendors at G05)
- Weather-dependent discoveries
- Walking-thought triggers
- Residuum mark placement points

Every location in the game rewards a visit. Not every location needs a mystery.
