## THE LEAVE LONDON ENDING

### Trigger
Available after: main storyline past midpoint (~5/8 nodes), Familiar with 3+ NPCs, Awareness Tier 2. Option appears in flat: "Pack your bags."

### Sequence

**1. The Decision.** "Leave London?" — "Not yet" or "It's time."

**2. The Last Walk.** Final walk through all three neighborhoods. No mechanics. Just walking. The pixel art at its most beautiful — current weather, time, season. Farewell thoughts (8 words max):
- "The canal looks different when you're leaving."
- "I'll miss the way the market smells."
- "The clockmaker's hands. I'll remember the hands."

**3. The Farewells.** One NPC per neighborhood — the player's highest-relationship NPC in each district. A brief exchange in their voice:
- Clockmaker: "The clocks will keep time. Come back." (8)
- Barista: "I saved your seat. In case you..." (8)
- Gallery Owner: "Leaving?" (1) — for once, not a question
- Warehouse Guard: "Go." (1) — containing everything
Others are simply absent. Absence IS the farewell.

**4. The Flat — Last Time.** All objects present. The notebook. The window. A held frame. The player taps when ready.

**5. Flashback Montage.** 5-7 snapshots, 3 seconds each. Theme music plays. Selection algorithm:
1. First NPC to reach Acquaintance
2. First lore fragment discovered
3. Highest-relationship NPC's Confidant moment
4. A difficult choice point
5. A discovery moment (hidden location or noticed detail)
6. The Watcher's first appearance (if applicable)
7. The flat window on day one — before objects, before the story

Each snapshot: pixel art still of the location at recorded time/weather. One line below (6-8 words).

**6. The Departure.** Train window. London receding. Pixel art simplifying with distance. Final trait-specific thought:
- Musician: "The city hums. I can still hear it."
- Photographer: "The light on the river. I see it."
- Wanderer: "Something is pulling. It always will be."
- Barista: "I know their names. All of them."
- Shopkeeper: "The ground remembers. Even if I forget."

Title card. Player name. Trait. Days in London. Save becomes a memento — viewable, not playable.

## EVENT ENDINGS (15 Total)

### Musician Endings

**M-END-1: "The Full Recording"** — Musician Climactic. Play London's complete acoustic memory. The mythological layer becomes permanently vivid for everyone. London's secret is public.
*Final:* "Everyone can hear it now. Everyone."
*Tone:* Triumphant and terrifying.

**M-END-2: "The Silence"** — XN-03, choosing to stop. Frequencies fade. The canal is just a canal. Everything goes quiet except: the barista's song survives. One melody. Everything else gone.
*Final:* "Quiet now. Just the river. Just the rain."
*Tone:* Melancholic, peaceful.

**M-END-3: "The Instrument"** — LI-01, entering and adding impression. The Musician becomes part of the recording. They continue living but are now audible in the archive. Future Listeners will hear them.
*Final:* "I'm part of it. Listen. I'm in there."
*Tone:* Transcendent, eerie.

### Photographer Endings

**P-END-1: "The Next Draft"** — Photographer Climactic. Document London's planned revision. The map of the future. Others will navigate what London becomes using the Photographer's work.
*Final:* "It's already changing. I saw what comes next."
*Tone:* Awed, urgent.

**P-END-2: "The Ordinary"** — XN-03, choosing to stop. The palimpsest fades to one layer. London is concrete and rain. But sometimes, in certain light, the shadows have too many edges.
*Final:* "Just a city. Just light. Just shadows. Almost."
*Tone:* Bittersweet.

**P-END-3: "The Archive"** — BI-02, four gallery pieces assembled. The composite image: London photographed from below. As if something beneath the city were looking up through transparent ground.
*Final:* "Some photographs are better kept in the dark."
*Tone:* Unsettling, profound.

### Wanderer Endings

**W-END-1: "The Waking"** — XN-02, entering the dream. London's consciousness encountered. A feeling so vast it has gravity. The Wanderer wakes London.
*Final:* "It opened its eyes. And I was inside them."
*Tone:* Sublime, dangerous.

**W-END-2: "The Dream-Keeper"** — XN-02, stepping back. The Wanderer becomes the guardian of London's sleep. Walking the boundary between dreaming and waking. They never leave.
*Final:* "I'll walk until it wakes. Or until I do."
*Tone:* Devoted, lonely, beautiful.

**W-END-3: "The Other Side"** — BI-10, following the Thames Path Figure beyond the map. London becomes entirely the mythological layer. The real city is gone. The dream is all there is. No return.
*Final:* "This is what London looks like from the inside."
*Tone:* Transcendent, irreversible.

### Barista Endings

**BA-END-1: "The Name Spoken"** — XN-01, speaking the name. Every NPC reacts. The network vibrates. The erased person doesn't return but the space they occupied is acknowledged. The Watcher is devastated.
*Final:* "I said the name. The city heard me."
*Tone:* Powerful, uncertain.

**BA-END-2: "The Name Kept"** — XN-01, staying silent. The knowledge is a weight carried privately. The hole remains. The Watcher's reasoning is understood, not agreed with. The name lives in the journal's last page.
*Final:* "I know your name. That's enough. I'll keep it."
*Tone:* Tender, solemn.

**BA-END-3: "The New Node"** — LI-06 resolution. The Barista has become the network's center — the person through whom all connections flow. They replaced what was erased. Not by choice. By attention.
*Final:* "I'm the one they all know. I'm the center now."
*Tone:* Quietly powerful, frightening.

### Shopkeeper Endings

**S-END-1: "The Awakening"** — Shopkeeper Climactic, using the key, touching the stone. The ground shifts beneath the entire city. The foundation is awake. It knows the Shopkeeper's touch.
*Final:* "The ground knows my hands. It moved for me."
*Tone:* Geological awe.

**S-END-2: "The Vigil"** — XN-03, choosing to stop but remaining. The Shopkeeper becomes the permanent thing — takes over the clockmaker's shop or the guard's post. They join the city's anchors. The next Shopkeeper will find the same key, decades from now.
*Final:* "Someone has to stay. Someone has to remember."
*Tone:* Committed, cyclical.

**S-END-3: "The Deep Layer"** — BI-03, spending the night, following the door to conclusion. Past Victorian, past medieval, past Roman. A living surface that responds to touch like skin to warmth. London's heartbeat: two beats per century.
*Final:* "I can feel every footstep. Every one. All of them."
*Tone:* Cosmic intimacy.

## FLASHBACK SYSTEM

### Significant Moment Tracking
The game flags moments when: NPC relationship advances, lore fragment discovered, investigation choice made, hidden location found, Watcher encountered, seasonal event experienced, trait stat crosses tier.

Each stores: location ID, time, weather, season, one-line description.

### Selection at Ending
5-7 moments selected for maximum emotional spread (not clustered), chronological distribution (early/mid/late), ending relevance (connected moments weighted), always including: first NPC connection and the flat on day one.

### Rendering
Pixel art still at recorded conditions. Character positioned. NPC present if they were. Below each: one fragment line (6-8 words). No animation. 3 seconds. Theme music.

### Autopilot/Agent Endings
Same structure. Autopilot: observed through text feed, may include moments the player didn't witness. Agent: recorded as journal entries, archived as found content.
